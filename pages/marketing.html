02 · Public claims and customer messaging

Marketing

How Zody AI Ltd keeps public-facing claims accurate, avoids misleading HMRC language and respects customer marketing consent.

Claims controlConsentBrand use

Questions this page answers

How are public claims reviewed?

Product claims, website wording and third-party listings are reviewed to avoid unsupported statements or misleading regulatory language.

How is HMRC language handled?

The business avoids claiming HMRC approval or accreditation unless that status has formally been granted.

How is marketing consent managed?

Personal data is not used for direct marketing without a lawful basis and, where required, clear customer consent.

Marketing controls

Zody AI Ltd aims to market its software in a factual and proportionate way. Public materials describe what the software does, the problems it is designed to help with, and the limits of any integration or status. References to HMRC-related workflows are framed carefully so they do not imply endorsement, recognition or approval unless such status has actually been granted.

  • No use of HMRC logos without clear permission and a genuine need.
  • No use of wording such as approved or accredited where that would be inaccurate.
  • Any customer data used for updates, newsletters or product announcements is handled under documented privacy controls.

Wording standard

Use phrasing such as “designed to support HMRC-related workflows” or “built in alignment with published HMRC developer guidance” unless and until a formal recognised status exists.
