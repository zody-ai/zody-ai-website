02 · Public claims and customer messaging

Marketing

How Zody AI Ltd keeps public-facing claims accurate, manages customer consent and ensures marketing communications are proportionate and honest.

Claims controlConsentBrand use

Questions this page answers

How are public claims reviewed?

Product claims, website wording and third-party listings are reviewed to avoid unsupported statements or inaccurate descriptions of what the software does.

How is regulatory language handled?

Where software integrates with regulated platforms, the business takes care not to imply approval or accreditation that has not been formally granted.

How is marketing consent managed?

Personal data is not used for direct marketing without a lawful basis and, where required, clear customer consent.

Marketing controls

Zody AI Ltd markets its software in a factual and proportionate way. Public materials describe what the software does, the problems it helps with, and the limits of any integration or third-party status. Where the software interacts with regulated platforms, we take care that external-facing language does not imply endorsement, accreditation or approval beyond what has actually been granted.

  • No use of third-party logos or marks without permission and a genuine need.
  • No use of terms like approved or accredited where that status has not been formally confirmed.
  • Any customer data used for updates, newsletters or product announcements is handled under documented privacy controls.

Wording standard

Where software integrates with regulated platforms, external-facing copy describes what the integration does rather than implying a status — for example “designed to support MTD workflows” rather than “HMRC approved”. This standard is reviewed whenever integrations or partner relationships change.
